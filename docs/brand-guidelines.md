# Brand Identity Guidelines: GroceryGenius

*Version 1.0 — June 2026 — For media, marketing, and partner use*

---

## 1. Brand Core

### Mission Statement
> GroceryGenius helps people make the most of what's already in their kitchen — turning everyday ingredients into great meals, cutting food waste, and making healthy cooking accessible to everyone.

### Vision Statement
> A world where no one stares into a full fridge wondering what to cook, and where good food at home is easy, affordable, and waste-free.

### Core Values
*   **Resourcefulness**: Start with what you have. Every feature begins with the user's real pantry, not an idealized shopping trip.
*   **Health without preachiness**: We grade recipes (A+ to D) and surface nutrition transparently, but the user always chooses.
*   **Accessibility**: Free to use, mobile-friendly, and fully available in six languages — English, Spanish, French, German, Japanese, and Chinese.
*   **Practical intelligence**: AI in service of dinner, not for its own sake. Smart suggestions, budget tips, and expiry alerts that save real money and time.

### Value Proposition
GroceryGenius answers "What should I cook tonight?" with AI-generated recipes built from the ingredients you already have — each with full nutrition facts, a health grade, budget tips, and an auto-generated shopping list for anything missing.

### Tagline
> **Stop wasting food. Start cooking genius.**

Supporting line: *"Turn your pantry ingredients into amazing recipes — powered by AI. Free to use."*

---

## 2. Brand Personality

### Brand Archetype
**The Sage** (primary) with **Everyman** warmth.

GroceryGenius is the knowledgeable friend in your kitchen: it knows nutrition, budgets, and a thousand recipes, but it never talks down to you. The "Genius" is in service of ordinary weeknight dinners — clever, not clever-clever.

### Voice & Tone
*   **Voice**: Friendly, encouraging, and practical. Confident about food knowledge, humble in delivery.
*   **Tone by context**:
    *   *Marketing*: Energetic and benefit-led ("Stop wasting food").
    *   *In-app*: Helpful and concise — short labels, clear actions.
    *   *Nutrition & health*: Factual and non-judgmental. A "C" grade is information, not a scolding.

**Do's and Don'ts:**
*   ✅ Do: Lead with the user's benefit — saved money, saved time, less waste.
*   ✅ Do: Use the full name "GroceryGenius" (one word, capital G's) in all copy.
*   ✅ Do: Keep all user-facing copy translatable — it ships in all six languages.
*   ❌ Don't: Guilt-trip users about food waste or diet choices.
*   ❌ Don't: Over-promise AI magic; the app suggests, the cook decides.
*   ❌ Don't: Write "Grocery Genius" (two words) or "grocerygenius" in prose (lowercase is reserved for the domain).

## 3. Visual Identity

### Logo Usage
*   **Primary Logo**: The 🧑‍🍳 chef emoji followed by the **GroceryGenius** wordmark in Nunito Black (weight 900), tight letter-spacing, set in white on the brand gradient or in Charcoal `#1f2937` on light backgrounds.
*   **Clear Space**: Keep at least the height of the chef mark clear on all sides.
*   **Misuse**:
    *   Do not stretch, distort, or recolor the wordmark.
    *   Do not separate "Grocery" and "Genius" onto different lines.
    *   Do not place the white wordmark on busy photography without the gradient or a dark scrim behind it.

### Color Palette

#### Primary — The Signature Gradient
The purple-blue gradient is the most recognizable brand element. Use it for hero backgrounds, headers, and key brand moments.

*   **Genius Indigo**: Hex `#667EEA` | RGB `(102, 126, 234)`
*   **Genius Purple**: Hex `#764BA2` | RGB `(118, 75, 162)`
*   **Gradient spec**: `linear-gradient(135deg, #667EEA 0%, #764BA2 100%)`
*   **Navbar (deep) variant**: `linear-gradient(135deg, #3D4FBB 0%, #4E2A82 100%)` — for sticky navigation and dense UI chrome.

#### Action Color
*   **Fresh Green**: Hex `#10B981` | RGB `(16, 185, 129)` — primary call-to-action buttons ("Generate Recipes", "Get Started"), paired with **Deep Green** `#059669` in a 45° gradient.

#### Secondary / Accent Colors
*   **Violet**: Hex `#8B5CF6` — feature accents, paired with Indigo `#6366F1`
*   **Berry Pink**: Hex `#EC4899` — favorites and highlights
*   **Amber**: Hex `#F59E0B` — tips, warnings, expiry alerts (with `#D97706`)
*   **Blue**: Hex `#3B82F6` — informational

#### Health Grade Colors (functional — do not repurpose)
*   **A grades**: `#10B981` (green) · **B grades**: `#3B82F6` (blue) · **C grades**: `#F59E0B` (amber) · **D grades**: `#EF4444` (red)

#### Neutrals
*   **Charcoal (text)**: Hex `#1F2937` · **Muted (secondary text)**: Hex `#6B7280`
*   **Card tint**: Hex `#F8F9FF` · **Lavender tints**: `#E0E7FF` → `#EDE9FE` (soft section backgrounds)

### Typography

#### In-App: System UI Stack
*   **Stack**: `-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif`
*   **Usage**: All product UI. Native, fast, and renders correctly across all six supported languages (including Japanese and Chinese).
*   **Weights**: 600 (semibold) for headings and buttons, 400/500 for body.

#### Marketing & Web: 
*   **Nunito** (Black 900): Display headlines and the logo wordmark — rounded, friendly, confident.
*   **Inter** (Regular/Medium): Body copy and UI text on the landing page.
*   **Playfair Display** (serif): Sparingly, for editorial accents and pull quotes only.

## 4. Imagery & Style

### Photography & Screenshots
*   Bright, appetizing, real-kitchen food photography — home cooking, not restaurant plating.
*   Product screenshots shown on the signature gradient or soft lavender tint backgrounds, with rounded corners and soft shadows.
*   People-focused where possible: families, weeknight cooking, grocery runs.

### Iconography & UI Style
*   Emoji are a deliberate part of the brand language (🧑‍🍳 ✨ 🥦) — use them in headlines and feature callouts, sparingly in body copy.
*   UI style: rounded corners (12px buttons), pill badges, gradient fills on key actions, generous white space.
*   Always mobile-first: assume small viewports in any mockup or asset.
